VIZ
Proof of Personhood, 2023
Singapore Art Museum

Support: Singapore Art Museum


VIZ departs from a series of promotional images from a “refugee simulation” at the World Economic Forum in Davos in 2010, where Facebook COO Sheryl Sandberg joined other thought leaders to role-play as a refugee.

In each photograph in the series, Sheryl's identity is combined with the biometric data of passports and identity cards obtained on the dark web, using a custom modification of a software package developed and disseminated by passport counterfeiters. Once a plausible hybrid biometric identity has been synthesized, the image is processed through a multi-layered generative adversarial network (GAN). An embedded face recognition model maintains fidelity to the previously-synthesized identity. This secondary processing “verifies” the synthetic identity—designed to pass muster under human and machine scrutiny alike—using the same techniques that transform the image into a photograph suitable for (human) viewing conditions.

Scrutinizing the images, the trained viewer apes the techniques of the border guard.

Hard Scrabble
062 Gallery
2018

Two forms of public sculpture punctuate the intersection of Roosevelt and Halsted Streets in Chicago. Inset from the street, James Turrell’s Hard Scrabble Sky perches on a foundation of stilts, framing, in Turrell’s characteristic style, an oval of the sky above. Streetside, the monopod of a ShotSpotter sensor projects away from the lightpost to which it is sutured, framing another waveform, the concentration and dissipation of sound below it. Both the ShotSpotter and the Skyspace (Turrell’s name for his signature interventions) interact as multiples, each one a component in a network of architectures that derive meaning and purpose through their iteration, through your knowledge of their iteration. What does their copresence, their adjacence, signify as a token of gentrification, of overpolicing? And how do the sculptures exist in a relationship of interdependence, one vision of a public, or a future, premised on the hearing of another?

Hard Scrabble
2018

Two franchises of perceptual technology articulate one another: anechoic experiments developed by James Turrell in 1968 while in residence at a defense contractor are scored for the ShotSpotter acoustic surveillance sensor that oversees his Chicago “Skyspace,” Hard Scrabble Sky. The ShotSpotter sensor and the Skyspace interact as multiples, networked architectures that derive meaning and purpose through one’s knowledge of their iteration—one vision of a public, or a future, premised on the hearing of another.

The scores are encoded for compact disc—the technological standard for courtroom evidence—and reproduced as embossings alongside documentation images.

Visual Inspection Zone
Proof of Personhood, 2023
Singapore Art Museum

Support: Singapore Art Museum


Visual Inspection Zone derives from promotional images of a “refugee simulation” at the World Economic Forum in Davos in 2010, where Facebook COO Sheryl Sandberg joined other thought leaders to role-play as a refugee for a day.

In each photograph in the series, Sheryl's identity is combined with the biometric data of passports and identity cards obtained on the dark web, using a custom modification of a software package developed and disseminated by passport counterfeiters. Once a plausible hybrid biometric identity has been synthesized, the image is processed through a multi-layered generative adversarial network (GAN). The GAN produces a new, high-resolution image from the network's latent space, maintaining fidelity to the previously-synthesized identity by way of an embedded face recognition model.

Through this secondary processing, the synthesized identity—designed to pass muster under human and machine scrutiny alike—is “verified” by the same techniques that transform the image into a photograph suitable for (human) exhibition viewing conditions.
